CS 581: Project 2 - Image Homography and Warping

Objective:

Apply geometric transformation concepts from class to perform image rectification and explore creative applications of image homography. The goal is to understand how 2D transformations can
recover fronto-parallel views of planar surfaces and be used in real-world applications such as document scanning or panoramic stitching.


Part 1: Image Homography Estimation

Implement homography estimation and image warping to rectify an input image containing a planar surface (e.g., a document, a poster, or a road sign) captured at an angle.

Tasks:

  • Manually select 4+ point correspondences between the original image and a target rectangular region.
  • Estimate the homography matrix H (e.g., using the DLT algorithm).
  • Warp the original image using inverse mapping to obtain a rectified (fronto-parallel) view.
  • Visualize before/after results.


Image rectification is the process of transforming an image to adjust the geometric distortions and cause the image to conform to a new coordinate system or view. Homography is a 3x3 transformation matrix allowing for the mapping of points from one image to another. It describes the relationship between two different perspectives of a planar surface.

Using a homography, a planar surface can be modeled (and/or reversed) causing a projective transformation of the camera view. As seen in the Rectified (Warped) images, I was able to pull the sides of the angled artwork to a forward view.
It worked very well. However, after the warping you can see the image is stretched compared to the original front view of the artwork

Triangular mesh warping is fast and simple to implement. The disadvantages are it is harder to get smooth curved shapes (which is noticeable in my results), and it needs good triangulation (triangle matching - however in attempt 1 for digit 3, the points being less matched led to a better result).
Thin-plate Spline warping works well with few points (I did try more with digit 3 and it made it much worse), is better for curved shapes, and there is no need to manage triangles. The disadvantages are fine-tuning small areas is difficult and moving one point affects the entire image The result for triangular mesh with my image of the letter A, looks better to me even though there is a hole on the right side.
